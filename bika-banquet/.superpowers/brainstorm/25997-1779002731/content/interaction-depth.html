How interactive should the calendar get?

Three levels — pick the one that matches what you need

A

Navigable + Hover-rich

Click any date → drill to day view. Click any booking bar → detail drawer. Hover on bars shows a rich tooltip (client name, hall, time, pax, status). Today button, keyboard left/right arrows. Month header shows booking count per day. Week cells show time-of-day at a glance.

Date drill-down Hover tooltips Keyboard nav Today shortcut
B

A + Visual polish overhaul

Everything in A, plus: richer venue sidebar (avatar-style venue initials, booking count badge), day view shows "Available" / "Busy" status per hall, month heatmap shading (deeper color = more bookings), week view shows time-of-day labels inside bars, pencil booking has countdown chip, occupancy % shown per venue in the header area.

All of A Venue avatars Heatmap shading Available/Busy pills Occupancy %
C

B + Drag / Quick-create

Everything in B, plus: click empty slot in day view → opens quick-create booking form inline (like Google Calendar). Drag a booking bar to reschedule (changes time). Drag to resize = change duration. Conflicts highlighted in red. This is the full Google Calendar experience.

All of B Drag to reschedule Click empty = create Resize = change duration Complex — weeks of work

My recommendation: B — gives a big leap in usability and polish without drag complexity. C is high effort, and for banquet booking (bookings are planned ahead, not dragged around) drag-to-reschedule may not justify the cost.
